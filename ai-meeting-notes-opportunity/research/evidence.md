# Evidence log — AI Meeting Notes opportunity

Accessed 2026-07-19. This is a bounded, public-web evidence log; it is not a market-sizing study. Quotes are reproduced exactly as observed, and each implication is deliberately narrow.

## 1. Otter — direct competitor

- **URL:** https://otter.ai/
- **Source type:** Official product page
- **Access date:** 2026-07-19
- **Supporting quote:** “Otter automatically generates summaries, action items, and insights your team can search, reference, and build on.”
- **What it implies:** A new product cannot differentiate merely by producing a transcript, summary, action list, or searchable meeting archive. Otter already presents that complete baseline.
- **Caveat:** This is vendor marketing, not an independently verified capability comparison.

## 2. Fireflies — direct competitor

- **URL:** https://fireflies.ai/
- **Source type:** Official product page
- **Access date:** 2026-07-19
- **Supporting quote:** “Get detailed notes, action items, and customized summaries instantly after every meeting.”
- **What it implies:** Automated post-meeting notes and action extraction are table stakes. The product positioning needs a workflow outcome beyond generation.
- **Caveat:** This is vendor marketing and does not establish adoption, accuracy, or willingness to pay.

## 3. G2 Otter.ai review — customer evidence

- **URL:** https://www.g2.com/products/otter-ai/reviews
- **Source type:** Public customer-review platform; review dated 2026-06-27 on the accessed page
- **Access date:** 2026-07-19
- **Supporting quote:** “The transcription is good most of the time, but it can struggle with multiple people speaking at once or strong accents. I usually need to make a few corrections before sharing the final notes.”
- **What it implies:** The handoff from generated transcript to shareable outcome remains a real job. A low-friction review-and-correction loop is more credible than assuming raw AI output is final.
- **Caveat:** This is one reviewer’s experience on a third-party platform; it should not be generalized into an accuracy rate.

## 4. Asana Anatomy of Work — collaboration workflow demand

- **URL:** https://asana.com/resources/anatomy-of-work
- **Source type:** Vendor-published research landing page, reporting a survey of 9,615 global knowledge workers
- **Access date:** 2026-07-19
- **Supporting quote:** “The higher up the corporate ladder one goes, the more collaborative work becomes—and the more problematic collaboration overload is for leaders.”
- **What it implies:** There is a plausible workflow problem around making collaboration legible and actionable, especially for people coordinating work. A focused meeting follow-through product addresses that problem more directly than a generic notes tool.
- **Caveat:** The page describes the **2023** report and is vendor-published; it is relevant context, not current independent market sizing.

## 5. Microsoft WorkLab — meeting artifact workflow signal

- **URL:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/worklab/
- **Source type:** Microsoft editorial/research hub
- **Access date:** 2026-07-19
- **Supporting quote:** “With Copilot, every meeting is a ‘digital artifact’”
- **What it implies:** Major workplace software is framing meetings as reusable knowledge objects, which supports a workflow built around retrieving and acting on meeting outcomes rather than treating a recording as the final output.
- **Caveat:** This is a WorkLab article title on a vendor hub, not a quantitative demand finding or an endorsement of this product concept.

## Evidence-bound conclusion

The evidence supports a **narrow GO**: pursue a differentiated validation of a reviewable meeting-to-work workflow. It does **not** support building a broad transcription platform, claiming a market size, or promising accuracy superiority.
