## ChargeMate

Best for: any processor

ChargeMate is the strongest choice for Shopify stores that accept payments through multiple processors — Shopify Payments, PayPal, Adyen, or any other gateway. Unlike Shopify App Store tools that are built exclusively for Shopify Payments or Stripe, ChargeMate handles disputes from any processor without requiring integration. Merchants upload dispute evidence directly, and specialists review every case, write a network-compliant rebuttal, and submit it on the merchant's behalf.

Pricing is straightforward: $10/case flat or 20% on wins — merchants choose. A free plan covers 3 cases per month, making it the lowest barrier-to-entry option for testing. Setup is same-day because no integration is required. The ~85% win rate reflects human review on every dispute, not automated template matching. For a Shopify store handling 20 disputes/month, ChargeMate costs $200 — compared to $600–1,200 at a 20–40% success fee model at comparable dispute values.

The limitation is operational: unlike fully automated tools, ChargeMate requires merchants to upload evidence for each case. This is a minor step — typically 5–10 minutes per dispute — but it means ChargeMate is not fully hands-off. The trade-off is full dispute coverage across every processor, and human expertise on every case.

## Chargeflow

Best for: Shopify-only stores

Chargeflow is the most prominent chargeback automation tool in the Shopify App Store. It integrates directly with Shopify and Stripe — pulling order data, customer information, and delivery confirmation automatically — then uses AI to draft and submit dispute responses without any manual involvement from the merchant. For a Shopify store that processes exclusively through Shopify Payments (powered by Stripe), this automation is genuinely compelling.

Chargeflow charges a success fee of 25–40% of recovered dispute amounts. At that rate, a store recovering $10,000/month in disputed revenue could pay $2,500–4,000 per month in fees. The model aligns incentives (they earn only when you win) but becomes expensive as dispute values grow. The fully automated, hands-off model is the key selling point — once connected, disputes are handled without merchant involvement.

The hard constraint is processor exclusivity. Chargeflow cannot handle PayPal disputes, Adyen disputes, or any processor outside the Stripe/Shopify Payments ecosystem. Shopify stores that accept PayPal at checkout — which is most of them — will find a significant gap in coverage. For those disputes, a separate solution is required.
