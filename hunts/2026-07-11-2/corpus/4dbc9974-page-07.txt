If you've been using HoneyBook and recently opened your billing statement to find a significantly higher charge, you're not alone.

HoneyBook's price increase in early 2025 — raising their Starter plan by 89% — sent a wave of freelancers searching for alternatives. Not because HoneyBook is a bad product, but because paying $36 or more per month for an all-in-one platform starts to feel expensive when you primarily use it for one thing: sending proposals.

This comparison isn't about bashing HoneyBook. It's about helping you decide whether a focused HoneyBook alternative like Winzi makes more sense for how you actually work.

## What Happened With HoneyBook's Pricing

In February 2025, HoneyBook restructured their pricing tiers. The Starter plan went from $19/month to $36/month — an 89% increase. The Essentials plan moved to $54/month, and the Premium plan to $81/month.

For freelancers who used HoneyBook's full suite — CRM, scheduling, invoicing, contracts, and proposals — the increase was annoying but arguably justifiable. The platform does a lot.

But for the large number of freelancers who signed up primarily for the proposal and contract features, the math stopped working. Paying $36/month for a tool when you're using maybe 30% of its functionality raises an obvious question: is there a better option?

## Who HoneyBook Is Built For

HoneyBook positions itself as a clientflow management platform. It combines:

- Client relationship management (CRM)

- Scheduling and calendar integration

- Invoicing and payment processing

- Contract management

- Proposal creation

- Workflow automation

This makes sense if you need all of these tools in one place and don't already have solutions for any of them. Event planners, photographers, and creative agencies who manage the entire client lifecycle — from inquiry to payment — get genuine value from the integration.

The challenge is that most freelancers already have some of these pieces covered. You might use Google Calendar for scheduling, Stripe or PayPal for invoicing, and a simple CRM or even a spreadsheet for tracking clients. What you're actually missing is a fast, professional way to create and send proposals.

## Where Winzi Fits

Winzi does one thing well: AI-powered proposal generation for freelancers and small businesses.

Instead of being an all-in-one platform, Winzi focuses on the specific workflow that wins you work:

- Set up your business profile — your services, positioning, and brand

- Build your rate sheet — your pricing structure, packages, and add-ons

- Brief the AI on a project — client details, scope, and requirements

- Generate a complete proposal — professionally structured, accurately priced

- Share with your client — via a direct link, with view tracking

That's it. No CRM. No invoicing. No calendar. Just the proposal workflow, done exceptionally well.

This focused approach has two advantages:

Lower cost. Winzi's pricing starts at $29/month — 19% less than HoneyBook's current Starter plan. You're paying for what you use, not subsidizing features you don't need.

Better proposals. Because Winzi's entire product focus is proposal generation, the AI output, pricing presentation, and sharing experience are more refined than what you get from a general-purpose tool where proposals are one feature among many.

## Feature-by-Feature Comparison

### Proposal Creation

HoneyBook: Template-based proposal builder with drag-and-drop editor. You select from pre-designed templates and customize with your content. Works well for simple proposals but can feel rigid for complex pricing structures.

Winzi: AI-powered proposal generation. You brief the tool on the project, and it generates a complete proposal with structured scope, phased pricing, and professional formatting. You review, edit, and send. Handles mixed pricing models (fixed + hourly + retainer) that template builders struggle with.

Verdict: Winzi produces more complete first drafts with less manual work. HoneyBook gives more visual customization options.

### Pricing and Rate Sheets

HoneyBook: Basic pricing tables integrated into templates. You enter line items manually for each proposal.

Winzi: Saved rate sheet that persists across proposals. Set up your pricing once — hourly rates, package prices, common add-ons — and the AI applies accurate pricing to each new proposal automatically.

Verdict: Winzi's rate sheet approach saves significant time if you send proposals regularly. No re-entering your standard prices for every new pitch.

### Sharing and Tracking

HoneyBook: Proposals sent via HoneyBook's built-in messaging or as a link. Includes e-signature and payment collection.

Winzi: Shareable proposal links with view tracking. You know when the client opens your proposal and how long they spend on it.

Verdict: HoneyBook wins on integrated signing and payment. Winzi wins on simplicity and tracking insights.
