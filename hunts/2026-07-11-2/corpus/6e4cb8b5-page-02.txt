## Executive summary

Now is a good time to build cheap EUDR compliance software for small businesses. The law is live, the deadlines are set, and most of the importers who have to comply are small. The enterprise tools are too expensive for them.

Keep it narrow and practical. Help small importers record where their goods came from, keep track of supplier declarations, and produce due-diligence statements ready for TRACES, all without needing a compliance team.

Two facts back this up. Small businesses make up 90% of the operators importing EUDR-regulated products. And their obligations kick in on June 30, 2026, possibly pushed to June 30, 2027 for micro and small firms.

The market is still moving, and that actually helps if you sell a guided product. Reporting starts before the final SME deadline, the scope is still being worked out, and buyers want help getting the work done, not a giant enterprise suite.

## Questions, answered

### My coffee shipment lands in two weeks. Can I file in time?

Yes. You request geolocation evidence from your supplier through a guided form, Traceply tracks what's still missing, and once the declaration is in you generate a TRACES-ready statement in minutes.

### Why not just hire a consultant for the first filing cycle?

EUDR isn't a one-off filing, it repeats with every shipment. We bundle onboarding for your first cycle plus an optional expert-review add-on, so you get the guidance once and keep the workflow forever.

### What happens if the EUDR scope changes again?

The product is built on modular rule logic and configurable data fields rather than hard-coded flows, so scope refinements become a settings update, not a rebuild.
