Marketplace and visual discovery channels can interpret product images differently, so sellers need a repeatable review process before publishing or refreshing a catalog. Treat channel-specific advice as a checklist to validate, not as a shortcut around product truth.

## What To Review First

Start with the product itself. Confirm material, color, scale, included items, packaging, variant differences, and any visual detail that could affect buyer expectations. If an AI-generated image changes any of those details, keep it out of the live listing until the output is corrected.

Next, review the page context. Product titles, short descriptions, offer text, reviews, and image captions should all describe the same item. A strong visual can still damage trust if the surrounding copy promises something the product does not deliver.

Product image decisions should be validated against catalog truth, channel requirements, and buyer expectations before publishing.

## A Safer Workflow

Use a small workflow before making catalog-wide changes. Select a narrow group of products, create image variants, compare them against the real item, review the product page, and document what changed. Keep the original image available until the new version has passed a human review.

- Check the hero image at thumbnail size and full product-page size.

- Review lifestyle images for product drift, added props, or misleading scale.

- Confirm that the image set matches the offer, variant, and fulfillment promise.

- Keep a fallback image for products where exact color or texture matters.

- Record the reason for every catalog-wide visual change.

## Pre-Publish Checklist

- The product in the image matches the actual item being sold.

- Color, scale, texture, and variant details have been checked.

- No generated scene implies an included accessory or feature that is not part of the offer.

- The title and description support the same product story as the image set.

- A human reviewer has approved the final image set before publication.

## FAQ

Should ecommerce sellers replace every old product photo with AI imagery?

No. Start with products where the current images are unclear, inconsistent, or expensive to refresh. Keep real photography for items where texture, color, or fine detail is critical to buyer trust.

How should a seller evaluate an AI-generated product image?

Compare the output with the real product, then inspect it in the context of the full product page. The image should make the buyer's decision easier without changing the product, offer, or expected use case.

What is the safest way to scale AI product visuals?

Use a repeatable review process, document approved image styles, and roll changes through a small product group before refreshing the broader catalog.
