If you are searching for EUDR software to help your small business comply with the EU Deforestation Regulation, you have probably already discovered two things: most tools are built for large enterprises and cost thousands of euros per year, and finding something affordable and self-serve is surprisingly difficult.

This guide compares the main EUDR software options available in 2026, explains what small businesses actually need from compliance software, and helps you decide which tool is right for your situation.

## What EUDR Software Actually Needs to Do

Before comparing tools, it helps to understand what EUDR software needs to do for your specific business. This depends entirely on your role in the supply chain.

If you are an operator — a company that imports regulated commodities directly into the EU — you need software that handles full due diligence. This means collecting GPS coordinates from source farms, running risk assessments, managing geolocation data, and submitting Due Diligence Statements to the EU TRACES NT system. This is complex, data-intensive work.

If you are an SME trader or downstream operator — a small business that buys products already on the EU market and resells or uses them — your obligation is dramatically simpler. You need to collect DDS reference numbers from your suppliers, store them securely for five years, and produce them on request. You do not need GPS mapping, satellite imagery, or TRACES NT submission.

The vast majority of small businesses affected by EUDR are in the second category. A coffee roaster buying beans from a Dutch importer, a chocolate maker buying cocoa butter from a Belgian supplier, a furniture brand buying timber from a German merchant — all traders with simple record-keeping obligations.

The problem: most EUDR software was built for operators, not traders. This means most tools are far more complex and expensive than small businesses actually need.

## The Main EUDR Software Options Compared

### osapiens Easy Start

Target market: SMEs and mid-market companies Pricing: Not publicly listed — requires a sales call Access: Demo request required — not self-serve

osapiens is one of the largest EUDR software companies in Europe. Their enterprise platform serves major corporations, and their Easy Start tier targets smaller businesses. The platform covers full due diligence, supply chain mapping, and TRACES NT integration.

The main limitations for small businesses: the pricing is not transparent, onboarding requires working with their team over several weeks, and their focus remains on companies with complex multi-tier supply chains. A small food brand with three suppliers would be paying for capabilities they do not need.

### Coolset

Target market: Mid-market sustainability teams Pricing: From approximately €12,000 per year Access: Sales call and onboarding required

Coolset is a sustainability platform that covers EUDR alongside CSRD and other ESG regulations. Their EUDR module handles operator-level compliance with supplier data collection, risk assessment, and DDS generation.

The limitation for small businesses is straightforward: the entry price is approximately €12,000 per year. This is appropriate for a company with a dedicated sustainability manager. It is not appropriate for a small coffee roaster or artisan chocolate maker.

### TraceX

Target market: Large enterprise operators in emerging markets Pricing: Custom enterprise pricing Access: Enterprise sales process

TraceX focuses on upstream supply chain traceability — connecting EU importers with origin farms in Brazil, Indonesia, and West Africa. Geospatial analysis, farm-level data collection, satellite deforestation monitoring.

This is not relevant for SME traders. This tool exists to solve the operator-level due diligence problem for companies sourcing directly from origin countries. Small businesses buying from EU intermediaries do not need this level of infrastructure.

### DataCross (tec4U)

Target market: German mid-market operators in automotive, manufacturing, furniture Pricing: Consulting plus software model Access: German-speaking market, sales-led

A German compliance software company with an EUDR module specifically for their existing customers in automotive, packaging, and furniture sectors. Requires engagement with their consulting team.

Not available as a self-serve tool. German market focus.
