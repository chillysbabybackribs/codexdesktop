## 1. W-9 Chaseboard

**Killer quote.** “Excellent for tracking 1099's required to be issued for my business and tracks receipts of the W-9's I received!” — [verified Etsy buyer](https://www.etsy.com/listing/4442461380/1099-nec-subcontractor-tracker-google)

**Quantified demand.** Etsy’s 1099 spreadsheet category showed **5,000+ items**; individual category listings carried visible review counts as high as **10.8k**. The exact CPA-created tracker above had one verified review at harvest time. Sources: `corpus/de55620b-page-01.txt`, `corpus/de55620b-page-02.txt`.

**Money row.** The reviewed tracker costs **$27.99**; adjacent bookkeeping sheets on the listing range from **$19.99 to $97**. Buyers currently pay once for static spreadsheets and separately for filing/accounting help.

**Incumbent and weakness.** Tax1099, Track1099, Avalara, and QuickBooks can collect/file tax data, but their center of gravity is filing or suite integration. The wedge is the messy pre-filing chase: request status, reminders, threshold visibility, payment-method exclusions, and an export-ready evidence trail for very small businesses.

**Two-sentence v1.** Import a vendor CSV, send secure W-9 request links, and show a traffic-light queue for missing, stale, or inconsistent records. Add payment totals and method flags, then export a preparer-ready packet without filing returns or becoming a tax system of record.

**First 100 users.** Bookkeepers already selling year-end cleanup packages, Etsy buyers/reviewers of 1099 trackers, and small-business bookkeeping communities in the October–January preparation window.

**Why now.** For payments made in 2026, the common 1099-NEC/MISC threshold changes from **$600 to $2,000**, while businesses filing **10 or more** aggregate information returns must e-file; the change makes old sheets easier to misapply. [2026 rules summary](https://quickbooks.intuit.com/r/taxes/small-business-guide-to-1099-form/)

**Confidence: B.** Kill it if 15 interviews show that existing accounting/payables tools already collect W-9s reliably before year-end, with no spreadsheet or email chase.

## 2. Review Cycle Builder

**Killer quote.** “Just what I needed to continue to develop our performance review process.” — [verified Etsy buyer](https://www.etsy.com/listing/1687857456/employee-performance-review-template-hr)

**Quantified demand.** The quoted template showed **7 reviews**, a **5.0** recency-weighted rating, and a **$3.56** sale price (**$5.48** original). The broader harvested Etsy categories contained many adjacent review-form and tracker listings. Source: `corpus/81144b68-page-01.txt`.

**Money row.** Small teams pay for low-cost spreadsheet templates or graduate to full performance-management suites. The opportunity is a modest subscription for the process between those poles: recurring cycles, private submissions, calibrated summaries, and printable records.

**Incumbent and weakness.** PerformYard, BambooHR, Small Improvements, and peers sell broad HR/performance systems; spreadsheets remain attractive because early-stage teams need a process before they need a suite. Zapier’s Small Improvements page explicitly surfaces Google Sheets/Docs connections, evidence that even suite users still bridge into documents.

**Two-sentence v1.** A manager chooses a rubric, schedules self/manager reviews, collects private responses, and receives a side-by-side calibration packet with unresolved rating gaps highlighted. No HRIS, payroll, engagement surveys, or goal platform in v1—just a defensible review cycle for teams of 5–40.

**First 100 users.** Buyers of performance-review spreadsheets, fractional HR consultants, and founder/operations communities preparing their first formal review cycle.

**Why now.** Structured document generation and summarization now make a narrow workflow materially more useful than a static sheet without requiring a full HR suite; the corpus also shows active automation bridges between review tools and Google documents. [Automation evidence](https://zapier.com/apps/small-improvements/integrations)

**Confidence: C+.** Kill it if template buyers consistently prefer one-off documents and will not pay even $10–$20 monthly for recurrence, privacy, and calibration.

## 3. 1071 Intake Lite

**Killer quote.** “Relying on manual processes to track potential lending disparities is unreliable, risky, and time-consuming.” — [Ncontracts N1071](https://www.ncontracts.com/products/1071-compliance-software)

**Quantified demand.** **Unquantified** in the harvested artifacts; the evidence establishes a live federal reporting workflow and an incumbent product, not a lender count or spend level. The CFPB’s current rulemaking page and small-business lending rule resource were captured as primary sources. [CFPB rule page](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/1071-rule/)

**Money row.** Regulated lenders already buy enterprise 1071/fair-lending software, commonly via quote-based sales. The narrow wedge is a low-complexity intake and validation layer for smaller covered institutions or consultants who otherwise assemble data manually before handing it to compliance counsel or an enterprise platform.

**Incumbent and weakness.** Ncontracts offers collection, reporting, analytics, audit trails, and fair-lending analysis—a strong product, but a broad enterprise-shaped scope. The exploitable segment, if real, is institutions needing guided collection, field validation, and clean export without buying analytics or replacing lending systems.

**Two-sentence v1.** Import applications from CSV, guide staff through missing Section 1071 fields, validate enumerations, freeze an audit snapshot, and export a regulator/vendor-ready file. Do not offer legal conclusions, disparity analytics, underwriting, or submission in v1.

**First 100 users.** Fractional compliance officers, community-bank and credit-union consultants, and small-business-lending operations groups approaching phased compliance work.

**Why now.** CFPB rule scope and compliance timing are actively changing, making field mapping and readiness a current operational project rather than evergreen admin. [Current CFPB rulemaking](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/rules-under-development/small-business-lending-data-collection-under-the-equal-credit-opportunity-act-regulation-b/)

**Confidence: B-.** Kill it if the smallest covered institutions all receive adequate intake/validation from their loan-origination system or compliance vendor at no incremental cost.
