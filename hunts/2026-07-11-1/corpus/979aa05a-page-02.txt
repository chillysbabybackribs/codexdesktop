## How this estimate is built

There's no universal price for a QuickBooks cleanup, but there is a sane way to estimate one. This calculator follows the same logic experienced bookkeepers use when they scope a messy file: start from the volume of work, layer on reconciliation time, then add a premium for the stuff that turns a catch-up into a repair job.

- Transaction volume × months behind is the engine. The single biggest predictor of cleanup effort is how many transactions you have to review, recategorize, and tie out — and that scales with both monthly volume and how far back you're going.

- Reconciliation time per account. Every bank and credit-card account is its own monthly reconciliation. Five accounts behind ten months is fifty reconciliations, not ten.

- Complexity flags. Uncategorized piles, duplicates, and a non-zero Opening Balance Equity each add real time because they require investigation, not just data entry. Complexity — not just backlog length — is what you're actually pricing.

The hour estimate is then converted to a flat-fee quote using your effective rate, with a sensible spread on either side so you have room to come in under and look good.

## What the numbers are anchored to

The output is sanity-checked against real-world benchmarks so it doesn't drift into fantasy. Industry guides put a typical small-business cleanup with under 12 months of backlog in the $750–$3,500 range, with larger backlogs running well past that. A widely used rule of thumb is that a month of cleanup costs two to four times a month of ongoing bookkeeping for the same client, and ongoing monthly bookkeeping commonly runs from a few hundred up to $1,500+ per month depending on complexity. Use this estimator's number as a starting point, not gospel — your judgment on the actual file wins.

## Hourly or flat fee?

Estimate the hours first, then quote a flat project fee. Hourly billing punishes you for being fast and makes the client nervous about an open-ended meter. Treat the hour figure here as your internal floor and the quote range as the number you put on the proposal. If the file turns out worse than scoped, that's a change-order conversation — bake a discovery step into your engagement so you're not eating surprises.
