## What is Buffer?

What is Buffer?

Copy Link

Buffer is a social media scheduling tool that lets you queue and publish content across multiple platforms from one place.

It's been around for over a decade and has a reputation for being the "clean, simple" option in a category where a lot of the tools are focused on adding more and more features for enterprises.

## My Experience with Buffer

My Experience with Buffer

Copy Link

When we first started making short-form video content, I was copying the same post into LinkedIn, X, Instagram, and TikTok manually. Everyday, I'd do it first thing in the morning.

Except, the sheer fact of having to open all of those social media tools meant I'd inevitably get sucked into scrolling here and there. Before I knew it, an hour had gone by, and I had a context switching headache.

It was at that point that I decided I needed a social media management tool or my head was going to explode.

When I was looking for that solution, I testedso manytools.Metricool(unreliable and disappointing),Hootsuite(clunky and dated), Later (sketchy customer support and glitches).

Buffer came out as the clear winner.

I ended up using it for about a year to schedule content andreally liked it.At the time, it was the most reliable social media scheduler we tested.

Posts went out when they were supposed to, the interface was easy to use, and it never felt like we were fighting the software to get content published.

I love using aesthetically pleasing software with a thoughtful user experience, and Buffer felt modern while the others felt dated.

That said, more recently, I switched our company over toOrdinal. Not because Buffer is bad, but because Ordinal is more for B2B teams and allows us to set up auto-engagement for our LinkedIn and X posts (which is where most of our audience is).

That said, I still recommend Buffer all the time and still think it's one of thebest social media scheduling toolsavailable.

## Who is Buffer For?

Who is Buffer For?

Copy Link

If you're posting to three different platforms and you've found yourself manually copying captions, switching tabs, and trying to remember which version went where, Buffer is for you.

Once I started using Buffer, I'd end up spending an entire afternoon scheduling a month of content. It was nice to get it all organized, and then forget about it.

Buffer gave me a single place to queue, schedule, and publish without logging into each platform every morning.

## Buffer is Not Best For

Buffer is Not Best For

Copy Link

If you're running an agency with multiple clients, this is where I'd pause. Buffer can absolutely handle multiple brands and accounts, but there are limitations.

A friend of mine who runs a social media agency uses Buffer for her own company's content because it's simple and fast.

But for client work, she uses a different platform to keep costs down (she has around 20-25 different channels connected at any given time) and to help her build more complex client permissions and approval workflows.

Also if you need to share campaign results with a client, you might find yourself layering in another tool because Buffer's analytics don't go super deep (unless you use the MCP).

It starts feeling like a scheduling tool that's been stretched into an agency workflow rather than a platform designed for one from the start.
