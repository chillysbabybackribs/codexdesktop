Fast Track DAP Migration Framework Here's how it works Fast Track is a structured, five-phase approach to reduce migration risk, remove low-value content, and launch on Whatfix with stronger governance and better long-term scalability. Phase 1 Objective and KPI alignment We start by understanding your current adoption goals, what is working, where the incumbent program is underperforming, and what outcomes the new program must drive. Migration should serve the business case, not just replicate old assets. Phase 2 Content audit and analysis We review your current content to identify what should be migrated, improved, consolidated, or retired. This cuts digital debt and prevents outdated or low-value guidance from following you into the new platform. Phase 3 Solution redesign We redesign key user journeys using Whatfix capabilities such as Smart Context and enterprise-ready targeting so the new experience is more scalable, maintainable, and context-aware than the legacy one. Phase 4 Build, validation, and stakeholder review Our team executes the migration and rebuild, then validates content through testing, user acceptable testing (UAT), and review with your SMEs, application owners, and program stakeholders. Phase 5 Coordinated go-live We enable the technical transition, coordinating with your IT team for disabling legacy scripts and activating Whatfix, while measuring performance against the success criteria defined upfront. For eligible Fast Track engagements, Whatfix provides complimentary software access for the remaining term of your current DAP contract to help you avoid dual billing during the transition. Final structure depends on the commercial terms and approved deal scope. Does migration mean simply recreating our current guides one for one? No. Fast Track treats migration as a redesign opportunity. We audit your existing content, remove what no longer adds value, and rebuild key experiences in Whatfix so the new program is more scalable, more contextual, and easier to govern. Will users lose access to guidance during the transition? Fast Track is designed to preserve business continuity. Our migration approach uses a parallel-run strategy so your teams can transition without leaving end users unsupported in live workflows. How long does a Fast Track migration take? Timelines vary based on the amount of content being migrated, process complexity, application environment, business priorities, and SME availability. That is why the Fast Track team scopes a migration plan around your specific needs instead of offering a one-size-fits-all timeline. How much effort will our internal team need to invest? Y our team will still play an important role in prioritization, reviews, and validation, but Whatfix handles the migration and rebuild work. That helps reduce the lift on your internal teams while keeping key stakeholders involved at the right stages. What information does Whatfix need to scope the migration? The Fast Track team typically scopes around your current DAP vendor, contract dates, application list, content volume, content types, process complexity, upcoming application changes, user base, and stakeholder availability. When should we engage the Fast Track team? Early in the evaluation process. Bringing in the Fast Track team sooner helps align on migration scope, timelines, technical considerations, and stakeholder planning before the transition begins. What happens to outdated or low-value content during migration? It does not need to come over by default. Fast Track includes a content audit to identify redundant, outdated, or low-value assets so your new Whatfix program starts cleaner, leaner, and easier to maintain. How does Whatfix reduce long-term maintenance effort after migration? Whatfix helps teams scale with governed content lifecycle management, contextual targeting, analytics, and AI-supported authoring and optimization. That gives teams a more sustainable operating model after the migration is complete. Do we need to migrate everything from our current DAP? No. Most organizations use the migration process to rationalize what should be rebuilt, improved, consolidated, or retired. Fast Track helps ensure you move the content that still drives value, not the content that has simply accumulated over time. Can Whatfix help us improve the experience during migration, not just move it? Yes. Fast Track is built around optimization, not simple replication. The goal is to help you launch in Whatfix with better user experiences, stronger scalability, and a more modern digital adoption program. Ready to make the switch?
