Open the live P&L Breaking - May 12, 2026 Amazon emailed third-party developers today confirming the planned Selling Partner API (SP-API) usage fees and annual subscription are cancelled , not just delayed. After careful consideration, Amazon will not move forward with SP-API usage and annual fees at this time. The original $1,400 annual subscription and $0.40 per 1,000 GET-call overage are off the table. From the agency portfolios we monitor, the cohorts most exposed feel it inside a billing cycle, not a quarter. What Amazon announced The May 12, 2026 email from the Amazon Solutions Partner team opens with the line "Our developer community is incredibly important to us" and confirms the company has decided not to proceed with the SP-API usage and annual fees. The note frames the decision as a response to developer feedback received since the original November 2025 announcement and the March 2026 indefinite delay. Amazon Solution Provider Services team email, May 12, 2026 - cancellation of SP-API usage and annual fees The cancellation covers two components that had been on the table: → The $1,400 per developer annual subscription for SP-API access → Usage-based overage fees of $0.40 per 1,000 GET calls beyond the included tier allowance For sellers, the practical impact is that none of the 5-15% subscription price increases that tool vendors had warned about for late 2026 are coming because of SP-API costs. Vendors that had already raised prices to "absorb" announced fees now have a hard question to answer. Amazon Solution Provider Services team email, March 2026 - indefinite delay of SP-API fee implementation What it means for tool vendors Vendors that already raised prices Several large repricers, inventory tools and analytics platforms passed 5-15% increases to customers between November 2025 and March 2026 citing SP-API costs. Those increases now look hard to defend. Expect renegotiation requests from agencies and 7+ figure sellers within weeks. Smaller and bootstrapped tools The $1,400 floor was an existential threat for solo developers and niche tool builders. The cancellation removes it. Expect the long tail of single-purpose tools (review monitoring, refund recovery, niche reporting) to keep operating without forced consolidation. Consolidation pressure The merger and acquisition narrative of the past six months was largely framed around shared API cost absorption. With fees off, the consolidation thesis weakens. Deals already in motion still close on their existing logic, but the "API tax" tailwind is gone. Roadmap reshuffle Engineering teams that spent Q1 2026 optimising call volume to fit Basic-tier limits get that work back as headroom. Expect more frequent data refreshes, deeper historical backfills and faster onboarding flows from vendors that had been rationing calls. What it means for sellers The headline is simple: no API-driven price increases are coming on your seller tools in 2026. Three concrete actions for sellers and agencies: 1. Audit recent price changes Pull every tool invoice from November 2025 forward. Any vendor that raised its rate while citing SP-API fees is now overcharging on a withdrawn justification. Ask for a renegotiation or a downgrade path. 2. Push for richer data refresh, not lower price alone The cancellation gives vendors API headroom. Ask for hourly refreshes, longer history and full marketplace coverage rather than only price cuts. The latter is fungible; the former changes how you operate. 3. Re-evaluate the consolidation case for tool stacks If you consolidated to an all-in-one platform expecting per-tool API cost pass-throughs, the pure cost case is weaker now. The product case (single source of truth, unified P&L, fewer reconciliation seams) still holds. Decide deliberately, not by default. What it means for the SP-API ecosystem long term Amazon still spends real money operating an API that handles roughly 58 billion calls a year across more than 1.6 million developers. Cancelling the fee structure does not make that infrastructure free for Amazon. It signals that the company has decided the strategic value of an open developer ecosystem (and the third-party seller flywheel it powers) outweighs the recoverable cost. That said, "at this time" is doing real work in the announcement. The SP-API fee proposal can return in a different shape: tier-only, write-call-weighted, or limited to the largest commercial users. Sellers and vendors should treat May 12, 2026 as a reset, not a guarantee. Frequently Asked Questions Common questions about this topic Expand All Is the SP-API fee cancellation permanent? Amazon's May 12, 2026 email says the company "will not move forward with the SP-API usage and annual fees at this time." The phrase "at this time" leaves the door open for a future proposal in a different shape (tier-only, write-call-weighted, or limited to the largest commercial users). No new schedule has been announced. Will my seller tools refund recent price increases? Refunds are unlikely, but renegotiation is realistic. Pull every tool invoice since November 2025. Any vendor that raised its rate while citing SP-API fees is now overcharging on a withdrawn justification. Open the conversation this month and ask for a renegotiated rate, a downgrade path or a richer data refresh in exchange. What about the $1,400 annual subscription? The $1,400 per developer annual subscription is cancelled. It will not be billed for 2026. If a future SP-API fee proposal returns, the structure may not include a flat annual subscription at all - tier-only or usage-only models are both possible alternatives. What about the $0.40 per 1,000 GET calls overage? The usage-based overage rate of $0.40 per 1,000 GET calls is also cancelled. Vendors that throttled or rationed call volume to fit Basic-tier limits get that engineering work back as headroom for richer data refresh, deeper history and faster onboarding flows. Does this affect the Amazon Ads API? No. The May 12 announcement covers the Selling Partner API only. The Amazon Advertising API is a separate platform with its own access model and was not part of the announced SP-API fee programme. How does Nova handle SP-API costs?
